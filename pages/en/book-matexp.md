**Matemáticas experimentales** is a mathematics outreach book published in the *Estímulos Matemáticos* collection, jointly edited by the Royal Spanish Mathematical Society (RSME) and SM.

Through hands-on, visual and interactive activities, the book offers a different way of approaching mathematics, connecting fundamental concepts with practical experiences that encourage curiosity, exploration and discovery.

**Summary:**  
In elementary mathematics, hands-on activities are strongly integrated into teaching because they help students visualize and understand concepts. In advanced mathematics, however, their presence becomes much more limited.

In this book we rediscover five key mathematical theories whose definitions and results are complemented by applications to everyday situations and teaching proposals that allow us to see and touch the mathematical world.

As mathematician Stanley Gudder wrote, "The essence of mathematics is not to make simple things complicated, but to make complicated things simple." In that same spirit, this book aims to turn complex abstractions into accessible knowledge.
